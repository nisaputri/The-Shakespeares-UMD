London October the 26 1700
We hear that the Count de Pratt Resident
here for the serene house of
Lunengburgh interceded on wednesday last
with the Queen for a pardon for mr
Lowen, and not prevaileing in that suite
desired tenn days reprieve upon which
her Maj<sup>ty</sup> declared that she was unwilling
to deny his Excellency any request but
the murther of mr Loyd was so inhumane
and barbarous and required speedy
atonement that she would not grant
him the least favour : he was executed
yesterday on Turnham Green and the
same evening hung up in Chaines:
he confessed the fact saying I am grieved
